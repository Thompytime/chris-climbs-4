Beauty and the Beasts!

Here are two lists. The list on the left contains some of the UK's tallest hikes and the list on the right contains some of the most explosive!

Ben Nevis, Scotland

Location:
Scottish Highlands
Views:
Panoramic views of the surrounding Highlands, including valleys, lakes, and nearby mountains.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike typically takes about 6 to 8 hours round trip.
Cost:
Free access to the mountain, but guided hikes typically cost around £30 to £50 GBP.
Difficulty:
The highest mountain in the British Isles, with popular routes like the Pony Track and the more challenging North Face.

Helvellyn, England

Location:
Lake District, Cumbria
Views:
Stunning vistas of the Lake District, with views of surrounding peaks and lakes, especially from the Striding Edge ridge.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike usually takes around 6 hours, depending on the route.
Cost:
Free access, with guided hikes costing approximately £30 to £50 GBP.
Difficulty:
Famous for the thrilling Striding Edge ridge walk, with some exposed sections and a steep climb.

Scafell Pike, England

Location:
Lake District, Cumbria
Views:
Dramatic views of the surrounding valleys, lakes, and neighboring peaks, including Wast Water, England's deepest lake.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike typically takes around 4 to 6 hours, depending on the chosen route.
Cost:
Free access to the mountain, with guided hikes typically costing between £30 to £50 GBP.
Difficulty:
England’s highest peak, featuring rugged paths, rocky terrain, and spectacular scenery.

Snowdon (Yr Wyddfa), Wales

Location:
Snowdonia National Park, Gwynedd
Views:
Spectacular scenery from the summit, with panoramic views of Snowdonia, nearby lakes, and the Irish Sea on clear days.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike usually takes around 5 to 7 hours, depending on the route.
Cost:
Free access to the mountain, but the Snowdon Mountain Railway to the summit costs around £20 to £40 GBP for a return ticket.
Difficulty:
The highest mountain in Wales, with diverse trails offering varying levels of difficulty and the option of taking the Snowdon Mountain Railway to the summit.

Mount Acatenango, Guatemala

Location:
Chimaltenango Department
Views:
The summit offers incredible views of the Fuego volcano’s eruptions and the surrounding landscape.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike typically takes 6 to 8 hours, depending on the route and pace.
Cost:
The cost can vary, but expect around $25 to $40 USD for a guided hike, including transportation and gear.
Difficulty:
A demanding hike to the summit with steep ascents and volcanic ash.

Cerro Negro, Nicaragua

Location:
León Department
Views:
Spectacular views of the volcanic landscape and nearby volcanoes.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike usually takes about 1 to 2 hours to reach the summit.
Cost:
The cost for a guided hike and volcano boarding experience ranges from $25 to $40 USD.
Difficulty:
A thrilling volcano boarding experience down the black slopes of an active volcano with volcanic ash and heat.

Mount Fuji, Japan

Location:
Honshu Island
Views:
Breathtaking panoramic views of the surrounding landscape, including Mount Fuji’s crater at the summit.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike typically takes 5 to 10 hours, depending on the chosen route.
Cost:
The cost for climbing Mount Fuji is about $30 USD for a climbing permit during the official season.
Difficulty:
A popular yet strenuous climb to Japan's iconic peak, with several routes to choose from and altitude challenges at 3,776 meters (12,389 feet).

Mount Telica, Nicaragua

Location:
León Department
Views:
Stunning views of the surrounding countryside and nearby volcanoes, including active craters.
Time Taken to Hike:
The hike to the summit usually takes around 3 to 4 hours.
Cost:
Expect to pay around $20 to $30 USD for a guided hike.
Difficulty:
A moderately difficult ascent to one of Nicaragua’s active volcanoes with steep terrain and great views.
